Part 2:

Standard physics regards the reference frame of the photon as invalid because of limits issue, seeing a time not flowing and 0 distance as an impossible limit case, but we challenge this and consider this as valid. In our view, this makes the photon an instantaenous relation between the emission and absorption event, so in the frame of the photons, both events occur "at the same time", but this raise the question as what happended "before" the photon existed and "after" the photon ceased to exist. In the frame of reference of the photon, there is no before and no after, but in other frames of reference, there's a time, and points in space time in which the photon didn't "exist" yet and points in time in which the photon no longer "existed", with big quotes around the notion of "existing", because some reference could not observe whether the photon exists or not.

Part 3: The two photons problem

In this part, we ask a simple question: We have to objects A and B. A and B each emit a photon towards each other at the same "time", which means there's a photon pAB travelling from A to B and a photon pBA travelling from B to A. Then in regular relativistic frame, we see each photon travelling form their source to their destination, which means that at some point in space time, pAB and pBA are at the same exact location (with probably a pointwise interference at this precise and single space time location).

But in the reference frame of pAB, pAB was emitted and absorbed at the same time, time doesn't flow, distance are 0. Same for pBA.

The problem is, how can we discuss about the relation between pAB and pBA : can pAB know about pBA ? It seems that it can know about the "existence" of pBA only at the space time location in which they meet.

Part 4: The sequence problems

In this part, we have two objects A and B, but a photon pAB is emitted towards B, then after the photon is absorbed, a photon pBA is emitted from B to A. In the reference frame of pAB and pBA, there's no time, but in any  relativistic frame which are not at the speed of light, the following sequence holds:

The goal of this discussion is to build an intuition about how to reason about multiple photons and events when allowing the reference frame of individual photons. The idea is that while spacetime is not usable in that reference frame (time doesn't flow, distances are 0), the structure of spacetime is preserved.
